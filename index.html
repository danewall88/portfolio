MyAMP

A mobile App developed for Android and Smartphone, that allows users to check and modify their KiwiSaver and AMP Investment fund balances. I was responsible for the overall UX Design of the app, that encompassed prototyping, wireframes, material design, user interface design as well as interaction design. This App is being developed currently and will be available for download on the play store or the app store by August 15th 2019. Please view my case study on this project for a more detailed breakdown of the UX Design Process.

CLIENT

AMP Financial Services

PRODUCT

Mobile App

MY ROLE

UX Analyst
Prototyping
Wireframes
UX Design
UI Design
Analytics

PROJECT SUMMARY

AMP NZ have recently seperated from their parent company AMP Australia. The most significant difference for AMP NZ in terms of this change, is that AMP Asutralia now manage the insurance aspect of the company which means AMP NZ have to redesign their digital customer facing products in order to relfect this change. MyAmp was something I worked on during my time as an intern with AMP.

UX ARTIFACTS

Logos provided by the client
Analytical data provided by the client
Imagery sourced by me and approved by the client
Assets were designed by me
The UI was designed by me
The design process was my own

APPROACH

Provide AMP customers an unforgettable customer experience through the development of a meaningful mobile applcation to better assist them manage their finances.

Project Goals

1. Develop an MVP that reflects their current product
2. Embed modern and custom functionality
3. accurately forecast future user behaviour to accurately allow for new features within the design

RESEARCH METHODS

User Research (Survey)
Analytical Data
User Acceptance Testing (UAT)
Market Research

KEY LEARNINGS

The largest takeaway point that I ascertained during this project, was the importance of truly understanding the problem from a users perspective. This project taught me that design should not begin, until the business requirements and the needs of the users are truly understood, before solutions can be put forth. The end result of not adhering to my own advice, can be wasted hours having to redesign a feature or element of the app because the solution does not truly solve the original problem.

FINAL DESIGN RATIONALE

The final design embeds some modern and cool imagery, which is a massive improvement on the current app. Within the app, the use of the colour blue denotes trust and security, a key brand value for AMP. Improved information architecture means the app could be developed at a lower cost and also ensured that users can complete their tasks within fewer steps in a more intutitive manner. Moreover, the app has been designed to allow for two key features to be added in the next iteration of the device.

PROJECT SUMMARY

NXT Step is New Zealands leading graduate job and internship website. Their current site is outdated from in terms of it's functionality as well as UI/UX components. The project brief was primarily aimed at giving the website a UX/UI overhaul yet remain true to the core NXTStep brand values.

Logos provided by the client
Analytical data provided by the client
Imagery sourced by me and approved by the client
Assets were designed by myself and another co-designer

The design process was my own

APPROACH

To gather useful insights on the current NXTstep experience from users, staff and analytical data, with a focus on mobile improvement to adjust to an evolving market.

Project Goals

1. Redesign Interface
2. Revisit UX approach
3. Invest significant project time toward mobile design

4.User test new prototypes

5. Include new and innovative features to engage new and current users

RESEARCH METHODS

User Research (Survey)
Analytical Data
User Testing ( Observations and Interviews )

KEY LEARNINGS

Despite the time spent reviewing analytical data regarding user behaviour on this project, the conversation for the desktop approach could have been more in-depth, we spent too much of the budget fine tuning mobile and I felt the desktop facelift could have been improved.

FINAL DESIGN RATIONALE

The final design included the implementation of several large scale UX additions such as a graduate calendar and improved search functionality. Moreover, a lot of focus aimed toward improving mobile view to align with the needs of users, the UI alterations were purely aesthetic and give the site a much more modern, easy to use and playful feel, a look designed to meet the brand requirements as well as the user.

I participated in the Hackathon 2018 competition with a group from Yoobee Design School, wherein we selected "TVNZ" as our preferred hack, a competition that we took 1st place in. I entered in Robert Walters 2018 Hackathon with a group of colleagues from Yoobee School of Design. The prototype that we pitched ended up taking 1st place. TVNZ wanted designers and developers alike to develop a new and innovative way for current and new users to discover the breadth of the content availble on TVNZ's Video Streaming on Demand Service.

4. Prototype ideas
5. User test
6. Confirm final design
7. Present

RESEARCH METHODS

KEY LEARNINGS

The importance of teamwork and good communication became very clear to me upon winning this comoetition. Our team communicated perfectly and worked really well together which was a huge factor in us taking out first place. I try to approach any group projects in the same manner in which I did throughout Hackathon because of this learning.

FINAL DESIGN RATIONALE

The final design was a well justified and researched prototype that truly solved the problem TVNZ were facing. We highlighted during the design process that a massive challenge for streaming services such as Netflix and TVNZ VSOD is around the personalisation of mass content, there is just so much content for users to go through we chose to approach this problem and use the solution as our final deliverable.

AMP Financial Services

PRODUCT

Internal Digital Platform

MY ROLE

Idea Generation
Prototyping
Wireframes
UX Design
UI Design

PROJECT SUMMARY

AMP NZ align themselves with large companies that pay their staff superannuation as part of their salary packages. AMP view these relationship as a key aspect of their revised business model, this project was aimed toward AMP solidifying their relationship with Air NZ through the development of a product that allows employees to view vast amounts of relevant information pertaining to their superannuation funds, the knock on effect would ideally engage and educate users.

UX ARTIFACTS

Logos provided by the client
Icons designed by me
Overall UX design process was my own
The UI was designed by me

APPROACH

Deliver a product that will improve the user experience for large scale AMP superannuation investors whilst simultaneously delivering a unique and innovative product that will be used my multiple brands and a design that allows them to add their own flavour to the interface.

Project Goals

1. Conceptualise a product to enhance any employer superannuation schemes.
2. Design and test prototypes on potential clients.
3. Take a modular approach to the design in terms of branding as this will be reused by other companies who want to embed their own brand within.

4. Enhance alliances with superannuation companies through this digital strategic approach.

RESEARCH METHODS

Information Architecture
CX Journey Mapping
Prototype Testing
Interviews

KEY LEARNINGS

This was my favourite project throughout my design career so far. My own design capabilities came unto their own during this project and I was granted a high level of autonomy, the final design of which was met with much praise from my peers. I love design.

FINAL DESIGN RATIONALE

Bearing in mind how important large scale superannuation alliances are to AMP, we can see the value in this digital platform very clearly upon navigation through the device itself. An array of interesting and relevant data visualisation that users would otherwise not understand or generally have access to have been provided in a meaningful and engaging context. Moreover, the modular approach and the use of drop shadow and aesthetic graphs means that other companies who want their own stamp on the device should they choose to adopt it, can do so, thus truly solving the requirements of the business.
